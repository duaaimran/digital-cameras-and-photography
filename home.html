Digital Cameras and Photography

Revolutionizing the Way We Take Pictures

In an age where everyone has a digital camera in their back pocket, we cannot fathom a world where photography is more complicated than a simple click or press of a button. However, about 60 years ago, photography was a lot more complicated, requiring a tedious development process.

Film photography was the main mode of capturing memories back in the 1960s. Where nowadays we don’t think twice about taking a picture, with film photography every photo is carefully and thoughtfully taken, making it a fulfilling hobby for many.

After the introduction of the Sony Mavica and Canon’s RC-701, handheld cameras began to dominate the photography market. However, due to their cost and poor image quality, they were mostly used by newspapers to cover events like the 1984 Olympics. As technology developed over the years, so did cameras, leading to the high quality tools we have today.

Point and Shoot Digital Cameras

The first point and shoot digital camera was released in 1990, the Dycam Model 1. With digital picture storage, the Dycam could connect directly to a PC for downloads, which revolutionized the way people took and stored photos. By the mid-90’s, even Apple entered the digital market with the Apple QuickTake 100 in 1994. Manufactured by Kodak, the QuickTake was the first colour digital camera for under $1,000, storing 8 images in its internal memory with a 640x480-pixel CCD. Further development would include the LCD screens, sound, webcams and compact cameras.

Vintage Y2K cameras like the point and shoot shown on the right are again becoming popular with Gen Z, due to their retro aesthetic. The ability of the point and shoot to take high-quality photos with minimal effort has made it a popular tool for Gen Z photographers, who are looking for a more authentic and nostalgic feel to their photos.

Digital Photography as a Hobby

Digital photography has become a popular hobby for many people, with the rise of social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok. The ability to take high-quality photos with a simple click has made photography more accessible to the general public.

The rise of digital photography has also led to the rise of digital influencers, who use their photography skills to create content for their followers. Many influencers use digital photography as a way to express themselves and connect with their audience.

With the rise of digital photography, many people have turned to photography as a way to relax and unwind. The act of taking photos can be a therapeutic experience, allowing people to express themselves and capture the beauty of the world around them.
